The Challenge

Keeping the field and the office in sync

At the scale of a national builder, the people on job sites and the teams in the office rarely share the same tools or the same view of what's happening. Information lives in scattered threads, emails, and systems, and the gap between the field and the office slows everything down. Field Echo was the V1 of an internal communications product built to close that gap — a single, company-wide place to keep everyone connected and informed.

Context

Built on Palantir Foundry

Field Echo was built on Palantir Foundry, with the company's data organized into a Foundry ontology — a structured model of the real objects and relationships the business runs on. Working in that environment meant the product wasn't just a communication layer; it was connected to the underlying data, which is what made it useful at company scale.

Outcome

A shipped V1, used company-wide

Field Echo went from concept to a launched V1 internal communications product for an enterprise organization — built on real infrastructure, grounded in a real data model, and put in the hands of the whole company. As a PM intern, I got to help define, coordinate, and ship a product that mattered to the business, which is exactly the kind of zero-to-one product work I want to keep doing.
